4 step reporting flow

  1. Researcher discovers and reports a vulnerability to bugbounty@chain4travel.com
  2. Eventually the report will be forwarded to hexens.io security team to triage the submission and assign its severity and impact levels. In case any additional information is needed, you will be contacted.
  3. If the submission is eligible for a reward, we will notify the reporter and begin working on a fix
  4. Once the fix has been implemented, we will notify the reporter and request that they retest and confirm the fix. Once the fix is confirmed, we will issue a reward and publicly acknowledge the hunter (if they have chosen to be credited)

The Scope

We are interested in receiving reports on the following types of vulnerabilities:

  • Blockchain-specific vulnerabilities (e.g. consensus attacks, transaction malleability, forced hard-forks, etc)
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities (e.g. reentrancy attacks, funds draining, funds freezing, etc)
  • Security vulnerabilities in our web platform (e.g. Cross-Site Scripting, etc.)

Out-of-scope vulnerabilities include those that:

  1. Are from the rest of the repositories in https://github.com/chain4travel/ that are not mentioned in the scope
  2. Are a result of user error (e.g. weak passwords)
  3. Are disclosed publicly
  4. Are a result of automated tooling without a clear chain of exploitation
  5. Duplicate: have already been disclosed to us
  6. Affect outdated or unsupported version of the software
  7. Are related to physical security or social engineering

Eligibility Criteria (What we ask of You)

In order to be eligible for a reward, you must:

  • Be the first person to report the vulnerability to us
  • Provide sufficient information for us to reproduce and fix the issue
  • In case of High and Critical severity vulnerabilities introduce a PoC (Proof of Concept) on a local environment mimicking the production˜
  • Not be a current or former employee of Chain4Travel
  • Pass a KYC when required

Important notices

1. Non-disclosure

We request that you do not disclose the vulnerability to any third parties until it has been fixed and we have had a chance to notify any affected parties. We will keep your submission confidential, but please note that we may need to share it with third parties (e.g. service providers) in order to fix the issue.

2. Responsible disclosure

We ask that all submissions follow a responsible disclosure policy. This means that you give us a reasonable amount of time to fix the vulnerability before disclosing it to the public. We will make every effort to fix the issue as soon as possible.

3. Response time

We will do our best to respond to submissions as quickly as possible. Our goal is to respond to all submissions within 14 business days. If your submission is eligible for a reward, we will notify you and begin working on a fix as soon as possible.

4. Changes to the program

We reserve the right to change the terms of this bug bounty program at any time. Any changes will be posted on this page.
